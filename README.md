# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer shopping behavior using Python to uncover key factors influencing spending patterns, product preferences, and purchasing behavior across different demographics and locations.

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib / Seaborn

---

## Objectives
- Understand what factors influence customer spending
- Identify top-performing product categories
- Analyze the impact of demographics and behavior on purchases

---

## Key Insights

### Spending Behavior
- Customers who make monthly purchases tend to spend more overall.
- Subscription status is a strong indicator of spending, with subscribers spending significantly more (~198 USD) than non-subscribers (~122 USD).
- Customers tend to spend more when discounts are offered, indicating that promotions increase average purchase value.

---

### Location & Customer Segments
- Phoenix shows the highest spending levels, particularly among frequent buyers.
- Chicago maintains consistently high spending across all purchase frequencies.
- Houston and New York show relatively lower average spending.
- Middle-aged customers (26–65) contribute the majority of purchases, making them the key revenue-driving segment.

---

### Age-Based Behavior
- Customers aged 65+ have the highest average spending.
- Younger (18–25) and older (56–65) groups also show relatively strong spending behavior.
- Ages 26–45 show comparatively lower average spending.

---

### Product Category Performance
- Clothing is the dominant category across all locations and age groups.
- Electronics and Accessories follow but at a significantly lower level.
- Footwear and Outerwear contribute minimally to overall sales.
- Clothing acts as the primary revenue driver, indicating strong and consistent demand.

---

### Seasonal Trends
- Customer purchasing behavior remains relatively consistent across seasons.
- Autumn shows slightly higher spending, but overall seasonal variation is minimal.
- This suggests stable demand patterns rather than strong seasonal dependence.

---

### Shipping Behavior
- Express and Standard shipping are associated with higher spending.
- This likely reflects customer behavior (high spenders prefer faster shipping) rather than shipping type directly causing higher spending.

---

## Overall Conclusion
Customer spending is influenced by a combination of behavioral and demographic factors, including subscription status, location, age, and purchasing frequency. Product demand remains stable across categories and seasons, with Clothing consistently driving the highest revenue. Additionally, discounts play a significant role in increasing average order value, making them an effective tool for boosting revenue.
